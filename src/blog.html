Development Blog

1st October 2019

This was the first allocated time slot in our timetables for the group project. We were introduced to each other for the first time and we were asked to carry out team building exercises, such as making the tallest tower possible with scrap materials and playing two truths and one lie. This established good relationships for the group and in turn created a well dynamic in the group. We came to the conclusion that we would have bi-weekly meetings where we can meet and update the group on any progression or issues. We decided that the initial meet up would be to delegate work and set tasks and the second meet up was to consolidate what work had been completed.

7th October 2019

This was the first submission deadline. We had to create a design alliance which stated what everyones intentions were and how they aimed to behave throughout the group process. This was also paired alongside a reflection report for the kick-off, which was our first meet up, a few days prior. We had to consolidate everything we discussed in the initial meeting and agree on a common understanding as to how we wanted this group to work well together.

11th October 2019

We had an upcoming submission deadline: the pitch for our game, and we needed to come up with ideas for our game. We met up and selected one team member to be the team leader, their role was to ensure any admin tasks were dealt with, such as booking library rooms, and delegate roles to individuals whilst mediating the teamwork and manage disputes if any were to arise. We also selected one team member to be the note taker and would add to the development blog after each group meeting.

Additionally, we spoke about our groups individual strengths and weaknesses to delegate different areas of work to different people. This was to ensure we result in very high-quality work, as people would utilise their strengths. We decided on two team members with good presentation skills to be the faces of the pitch, and the group worked collaboratively on creating a script. The pitch was filmed and submitted successfully.

16th October 2019 : 10th November 2019

We had another meeting to begin our work for the third submission on the 10th of November, which includes The Hack, the Project Website, the Game Design Document and the Software Quality Strategy Report. We decided to get the team members to put themselves up for specific roles and the section they favoured the most. This was to ensure people worked on what they wanted to. Anything that was not selected was split up and distributed between the group. We created four teams, one for each submission task. A spreadsheet was created breaking up each task into individuals steps and this was done to ensure we have good time management and to make sure every single task was completed successfully. We utilised the application Trello to track all the task progression.

We met up twice a week till our deadline to maintain the good standard and prepare ourselves for the submission date.

20th November 2019

The next submission we had was the Minimal Viable Product and the Project Website and Development Blog. This was for the 12th of December 2019. This submission was split into two different subsections; the coding and the website. We decided to meet up and designate different roles to different members in the group, three members focused on finalising the game mechanics, two members focused on the website and making it look professional and informative, two members focused on getting the sound and graphics completed on the game, and one member on the development website.

This submission was a difficult one for the team due to the time constraints. We needed to complete a large number of tasks in a short duration of time, with the additional stress of completing work for the other modules simultaneously. This aspect of multi-tasking and prioritising tasks was difficult for us all to grasp. We overcame this issue by scheduling meetings early in the morning to discuss what needed to be done and allowing the team to go off independently and do their own thing. This worked well for us as we all managed to complete our individual roles in time.

5th December 2019

We continued to work towards our next submission, which was the: MINIMUM VIABLE PRODUCT. This was a small less complex version of our final product; it should contain all the core functionalities of the game and have complete working parts for the end-user. This MVP submission also included a up-to-date development blog alongside a fully working website.

We split our team into three different groups: those working on the game itself, those working on the website and those working on the presentation.

This meant that we had to utilise our GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT that we had produced for the previous submission task and construct our MVP through the use our HACK. Our hack was successful therefore in order to create the MVP was fairly simple as we had a base which we simply needed to adapt and add to. The game design document consisted of every feature we planned to execute in our final game and as the MVP is meant to contain the core functionalities of the game, we decided to pick the main components and implement that into our updated game.

The MVP submission was a presentation and in order to prepare, two of our group members had to come up with a script and plan a live demonstration of our game.

18th February 2020

We had a scheduled lecture with Peter. The purpose of the lecture was to get us to reflect on our team.

We were instructed to focus on the positives of our team and the steps we take which are sucessful and are beneficial to the teams progess. We drew out a ship and above the ship were the positives which helped the sails move forward. We recognised our commitment, leadership and attitude towards one another was what allowed us to succeed.

When looking at our downfall; the areas in which we found we were weighing our boat down was our like of time management, attitude towards the tasks and our lack of organisation. These steps discouraged us to work to our best abilities and simultaneously led to poor and low quaity work.

We finally reflected back on what we would do differently and how we could avoid any of the negative steps we had taken in the past and came up with steps we could take from this moment forward to increase momentum in our progress. These steps included, setting regular meetings every week with both the subteams and the entire team, establishing timely goals to ensure the final deadline would be completed sucessfully, and encourage more team communication throughout.

21st March 2020

The next deadline we had was the SCREENCAST task. This task involves a screencast that demonstrates the software running. It should be targeted at your target users/players and be no longer than 15 minutes. It should be narrated, to explain what is being shown.

Therefore, in order to complete this task, we had to split the team, yet again, into subgroups. This was to ensure each section of the task was completed to a high standard. We scheduled in multiple DISCORD meetings in which we were able to delegate roles and update the group on the individual progess.

We set up a meeting in which we identified the positives of our game so far, the ways we could improve our game now, and the issues we faced alongside the solutions on how we would fix it. This task was very useful as it allowed us to identify key points of our game that needed attending to and additionally highlight the areas which we had successfully completed.
