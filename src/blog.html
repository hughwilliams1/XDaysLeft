Development Blog

1st October 2019

This was the first allocated time slot in our timetables for the group project. We were introduced to each other for the first time and we were asked to carry out team building exercises, such as making the tallest tower possible with scrap materials and playing two truths and one lie. This established good relationships for the group and in turn created a well dynamic in the group. We came to the conclusion that we would have bi-weekly meetings where we can meet and update the group on any progression or issues. We decided that the initial meet up would be to delegate work and set tasks and the second meet up was to consolidate what work had been completed.

7th October 2019

This was the first submission deadline. We had to create a ‘design alliance’ which stated what everyone’s intentions were and how they aimed to behave throughout the group process. This was also paired alongside a reflection report for the kick-off, which was our first meet up, a few days prior. We had to consolidate everything we discussed in the initial meeting and agree on a common understanding as to how we wanted this group to work well together.

11th October 2019

We had an upcoming submission deadline – the pitch for our game – and we needed to come up with ideas for our game. We met up and selected one team member to be the team leader, their role was to ensure any admin tasks were dealt with, such as booking library rooms, and delegate roles to individuals whilst mediating the teamwork and manage disputes if any were to arise. We also selected one team member to be the ‘note taker’ and would add to the development blog after each group meeting.

Additionally, we spoke about our group’s individual strengths and weaknesses to delegate different areas of work to different people. This was to ensure we result in very high-quality work, as people would utilise their strengths. We decided on two team members with good presentation skills to be the faces of the pitch, and the group worked collaboratively on creating a script. The pitch was filmed and submitted successfully.

16th October 2019 – 10th November 2019

We had another meeting to begin our work for the third submission on the 10th of November, which includes The Hack, the Project Website, the Game Design Document and the Software Quality Strategy Report. We decided to get the team members to put themselves up for specific roles and the section they favoured the most. This was to ensure people worked on what they wanted to. Anything that wasn’t selected was split up and distributed between the group. We created four teams, one for each submission task. A spreadsheet was created breaking up each task into individuals’ steps and this was done to ensure we have good time management and to make sure every single task was completed successfully. We utilised the application ‘Trello’ to track all the task progression.

We met up twice a week till our deadline to maintain the good standard and prepare ourselves for the submission date.

11th October 2019

The next submission we had was the Minimal Viable Product and the Project Website and Development Blog. This was for the 12th of December 2019. This submission was split into two different subsections; the coding and the website. We decided to meet up and designate different roles to different members in the group, three members focused on finalising the game mechanics, two members focused on the website and making it look professional and informative, two members focused on getting the sound and graphics completed on the game, and one member on the development website.

This submission was a difficult one for the team due to the time constraints. We needed to complete a large number of tasks in a short duration of time, with the additional stress of completing work for the other modules simultaneously. This aspect of multi-tasking and prioritising tasks was difficult for us all to grasp. We overcame this issue by scheduling meetings early in the morning to discuss what needed to be done and allowing the team to go off independently and do their own thing. This worked well for us as we all managed to complete our individual roles in time.
